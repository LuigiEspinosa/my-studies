- Anchoring: I's an art of how you ask the question, how you set the anchor.
- Decoy Effect: Consumers will tend to have a specific change in preference between two options when also presented with a third option that is asymmetrically dominated.
- Status Quo: Keep the things the way they presently are.
- Goal Gradient: If there is a progress, we tend to finish it.
- Expectancy Effect: People tend to find evidence to things they believe true.
- Framing: It's an inevitable process of selective influence over the individual's perception of the meanings attributed to words or phrases.
- Decision Point Intercepts: The most critical time to affect a customer's decision is at the time he is making it.
- Planning Fallacy: Optimism bias makes any human based estimation is always more optimistic than it should be.
- Mental Accounting: People split up their future and current assets into separate portions that cannot be transferred.
- Sunk Cost Effect: The sunk cost effect is the tendency for humans to continue investing in something that clearly isn't working. Just because they invested an initial.
- Choice Overload: Many choices cause complicated computation resulting with psychological pressure. One of the results of this pressure is regret.
- Joint & Separate Evaluation: When several products are compared to each other simultaneously, it is called the joint evaluation and when they are compared at different contexts, it is called the separate evaluation.

## Senses

### Scent Marketing

75% of the emotions we generate on a daily basis are affected by smell. Next to sight, it is the most important sense we have. We link smell with pleasant or unpleasant experiences instantaneously, bringing subconscious levels of string nostalgia. Smell works on such a subconscious level, it is more effective in triggering an impulsive reaction.

- Aroma Billboard Smell: The customers are consciously aware of it, such as the trademark smell of freshly brewed coffee at Starbucks.
- Thematic Smell: Complements the décor or purpose of the place, such as vanilla or lavender smells at a beauty spa or a resort.
- Ambient Smells: More subtle and create an atmosphere, used to cover unpleasant odours or to "fill a void".
- Signature Smell: Exclusive to a big brand name and is used to create a brand image.

Effect of smell is affected by everything from cultural norms, gender and appropriateness to the product.

## Psychological Tricks in Real Life

When you try to sell a service, you need to sell yourself before trying to sell the service. It all depends how much your client trusts and likes you.

- Copy Them: This strategy is called mirroring, and involves subtly mimicking the other persons's behavior.
- Spend more time around them: According to the mere-exposure effect, people tend to like things that are familiar to them.
- Compliment: People will associate the adjectives you use to describe other people with your personality. This phenomenon is called spontaneous trait transference.
- Emotional contagion describes what happens when people are strongly influenced by the moods of other people.
- The social-network theory behind this effect i called triadic closure, which means that two people are likely to be closer when the have a common friend.
- The gain loss theory of interpersonal attractiveness suggest that your positive comments will make more of an impact if you deliver them only occasionally.
