Self-awareness allows leaders to understand their strengths, limitations, values and emotional triggers, and when leaders understand this, it enables them to be more authentic, intentional and empathetic.

High self-awareness often goes hand in hand with high levels of trust as well. if leaders open up about their challenges and play to their strengths, it will encourage others to do the same.

Without self-awareness. even well-meaning leaders can fall short, overlooking how their actions affect others, unintentionally knocking confidence, or shutting down new ideas. The best leaders don't pretend to have all the answers. They listen. They reflect. The grow. And in doing so, they give others permission to do the same.

## Own Your Self

1. Schedule self-reflection time.
2. Trust the power of a pause.
3. Identify your stress signals.
4. Celebrate your wins.
5. Find your *centre*.
6. Set intentions.
7. Cultivate gratitude.
