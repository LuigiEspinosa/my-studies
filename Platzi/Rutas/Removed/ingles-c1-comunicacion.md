## Introduction to Rhetoric

> "Rhetoric is an art of ruling the minds of man" - Plato

Rhetoric is the art of speaking or writing persuasively. Aim - to persuade, motivate and influence your audience.

- **Judicial Rhetoric:** Used in court by lawyers and judges - Justice / Injustice of accusation.
- **Demonstrative Rhetoric:** Used to give praise (or blame) - wedding / retirement speeches.
- **Deliberative Rhetoric:** Used to initiate a call to action - leaders use it to influence & motivate.

### Things to consider

- Analyze the situation.
- The rhetor (yourself).
- The audience.
- The topic.
- The purpose (Entertain, influence, motivate, call to action, etc).

The three pillars of rhetoric are:

1. Ethos - Ethical Side
    - To build trust, respect, show expertise & authority.
    - To be established in the beginning of your discourse.
    - To use personal stories, facts, statistics & unbiased opinions.

2. Pathos - Emotional Side
    - Appealing to audience's emotions.
    - Getting your arguments accepted & acting on call to action.
    - Using metaphors, analogies, storytelling, emotionally charged vocabulary, etc.

3. Logos - Logical Side
    - Showing evidence to support your arguments.
    - Using figures, charts, and exploiting facts to make connections yo your arguments.
    - Citing specialists, case studies, visual evidence (photos/videos), etc.

You can use rhetorical devices to support the three pillars. Presenting facts & accepting counter arguments (unbiased). Using classifications & division (break down things), personal stories & statistics. Purpose = to build credibility & trust (Ethos).

Tell a story, who, what, when, where & results. Using body language/voice to match your words. Purpose = to invoke emotions (Pathos).

Use evidence & examples. Defining something for your audience, showing clear steps to explain process. Purpose = to get your audience to see your point of view (Logos).

## Rhetorical Tools and Devices

### The rule of three

- The rule of 3 is here, there & everywhere
- Information presented in a list of 3 makes our message more memorable, understandable & even enjoyable.
- Very common rhetorical device used by advertising, lawyers and leaders.

### Anaphora (Repetition)

- Repeating a word or phrase 3 times in the beginning of a sentence.
- Anaphora allows us to highlight that word/phrase, catch our audiences' attention and make our message memorable.

### Balanced statements

- Instead of presenting information in a single sentence, _we form sentences in two parts that balance._
- We like balance in our lives - work-life balance, mind-body, emotional-spiritual, etc.

### Antanagoge

- If you have to say something negative, follow it with something positive.
- This allows us to reduce the impact created from the negative information.
- Human beings naturally associate more with positive things and disassociate from the negative.

### Metaphors

- We make comparison by saying something is like another.
- Easier for your audience to visualice what you are trying to say.
- It's almost like _projecting your thoughts on a screen so your audience can see them_

### Parallelism

- Achieved by using similar grammatical components in your sentence.
- Used to achieve a flow in your message.
- It can be used in _writing, speaking and creating_ meaningful messages.

### Hyperbole (Exaggeration)

- Exaggeration is used to create a powerful impact on your audience (Don't over-do it).
