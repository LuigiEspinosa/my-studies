## Have vs. Have got

Both means "tener", but:

- *Have got* can be use just in simple present, for the other ones use *have*.
- *Have got* is mostly use for British English speakers.
- *Have* has another meanings like "tomar", "haber".
- *Have got* has a different negative and interrogative form.

![Have got Grammar](https://static.platzi.com/media/user_upload/10-22%20%282%29%20clase%20have%20got-baaf6584-ba4a-463c-bee8-6eb75dd8bbf3.jpg)

## Want and Wishes

When we are talking about things we hope to do in the future, **want and would like** have the same meaning: “I would really **like** to go to Brazil, one day.” “I really **want** to go to Brazil one day.” However, when we are talking to other people in formal situations, **would like** is more polite than want.

## There is vs. There are

- Use _there is_ when the noun is singular (“There is a cat”). Use _there are_ when the noun is plural (“There are two cats”).
